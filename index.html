The Surveillance Spike

How Camera Technology Transformed Traffic Enforcement in Australia (2008-2024)

The Story at a Glance

Between 2008 and 2024, Australian traffic enforcement underwent a dramatic transformation. The introduction of automated camera detection systems didn't just supplement traditional policing it fundamentally changed the scale and nature of mobile phone enforcement.

Key Insight

In NSW, fines increased from 25,463 in 2019 to 138,847 in 2020 — a 445% jump following the introduction of mobile phone detection cameras.

The Shift in Enforcement: Man vs Machine

Automated cameras have fundamentally changed who catches mobile phone users. What took thousands of officers years to accomplish, cameras can now do in months.

The Efficiency Gap

In Queensland 2024, cameras issued 28,641 fines to drivers aged 40-64, while police issued just 849 to the same group—a 33:1 ratio.

Interpretation: The stacked areas show the proportion of detections by method. Watch how the blue (Police) area remains relatively constant while the orange (Camera) area explodes from 2020 onwards.

Demographic Risk Profiles

Not all age groups or locations are caught equally. Younger drivers and those in major cities represent the highest risk categories.

Key Finding: In NSW Major Cities (2024), the 17-25 and 26-39 age groups had 4,539 and 4,401 fines respectively across all location types.

The Scale of the Shift

Before cameras, annual national fines peaked around 170,000. By 2023, that figure had grown to over 390,000 — driven almost entirely by automated detection.
